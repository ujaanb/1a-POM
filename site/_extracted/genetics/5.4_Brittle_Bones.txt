    5.4 Brittle Bones

           What is a special characteristic of the amino acid cysteine?
             •   It can form disulphide bridges between two of its residues which can
                 lead to the linkage seen

           What consequences could result from the change of amino acid residue
              glycine to cysteine in collagen?
             •  Glycine is the smallest amino acid as it can fit down the middle of the
                 collagen triple helix
             •  Changing this to cysteine could result in improper assembly of the
                    triple helix hence giving the collagen poor functionality as it cannot
                   fold well into fibres.

         Why does heterozygosity for mutated collagen a1 chain not just result in
         50% of alpha chains to be abnormal?
             •   It should be like this in an ideal world, but there are so many factors
                   that play a part here
             •  There may be differences in the rates of transcription of the gene,
                  rate of translation, stability of mRNA or stability of the protein which
                 lead to a different ratio of abnormal and normal chains.

         How does abnormal collagen type I affect the formation of bone?
             • The bone is formed when hydroxyapatite (form of calcium
               phosphate) is laid down upon a collagen type I scaffold
             • The abnormal collagen type I leads to defects in the mineralisation
               process, which ultimately results in weak bones.

           What other parts of the body can be affected by abnormal collagen
             formation?
             •  Eyes
             •  Teeth
             •  Skin
             •  Ears

             Describe a prenatal technique that can be used to detect genetic
              abnormalities (i.e mutations).
             •  Chorionic villus sampling or amniocentesis can be used to obtain the
            DNA
             • PCR can be used to amplify the DNA, and analyse the abnormalities
             • A limitation is that we have to know the existing mutation in order to be
                 able to test for it (i.e adding a probe to see how the DNA interacts with
                            it etc.)

           What is restriction fragment length polymorphism (RFLP)?
             • An RFLP probe is a labeled DNA sequence that hybridises with one
                 or more fragments of the digested DNA sample after they were
                separated by gel electrophoresis, thus revealing a unique blotting
                  pattern characteristic to a specific genotype at a specific locus.
