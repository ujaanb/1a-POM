           What special component do viruses with RNA instead of DNA have?
             •  Reverse transcriptase enzymes to help them convert RNA back to
              DNA.

         Why do RNA viruses have a higher mutation rate than DNA viruses?
             • RNA viruses use their own polymerases to replicate which leads to
                    this higher rate of mutation
                 ◦ DNA viruses use host cell machinery
             •   It is an advantageous characteristic, as the viruses can evolve
                  quicker.

           What other distinction is there between DNA and RNA viruses?
             • DNA viruses are much larger than RNA viruses
             • RNA viruses are around 30 kb pairs big, DNA viruses are about 100kb
                   pairs big.

           What is negative sense RNA?
             •  This is RNA derived from the complimentary strand to the mRNA
                 strand (so tRNA)
             •  Viruses that carry this form of RNA have to convert the tRNA into
             mRNA, which can then be translated

           What is the role of an integrase enzyme in the viral replication cycle?
             •  Integrases are used to incorporate the viral DNA into the host cell
            DNA genome, so that it can be transcribed together and produce the
                      viral proteins.

           What is the negative sense strand in RNA?
             • The strand that is complementary to mRNA
             • mRNA can be synthesised using the negative sense strand.
           What is the cytopathic effect?
             • The death of a host cell caused by a virus when it replicates and
               causes lysis/apoptosis of the host cell.

           What is a plaque?
             •   It is a hole in a layer of cells that is caused by the action of a virus
             when it kills a host cell
             • The hole is present due to the absence of the cell that was previously
                     in that position.
