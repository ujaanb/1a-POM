    4.12 Diagnostic Virology II

              Recall some viral infections that can be sexually transmitted.
             •  HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus)
             • HTLV (Human T-Lymphocytic Virus)
             •  Hepatitis B/C
             • Human Papilloma Virus
             •  Herpes Simplex Virus
           What is point-of-care testing?
             •  Carrying out a test using a device or test kit in the presence of the
                 patient and without the need to send a sample to a laboratory.

           What may be the reasons behind a HIV test returning negative?
             • The patient actually does not have HIV
             • The test has been performed too close to the onset of the infection,
               so there are not enough HIV antigens present yet to be detected
             • The test has been performed too close to the onset of the infection,
               so there are not enough HIV antibodies present to be detected

           Which further tests can be done beyond an antigen test for HIV?
             •  Antibody test
             • PCR to amplify blood samples
             •  Nucleic acid tests (NAT) done to detect RNA in actual HIV.
             •  qrt-PCR or RT-qrt-PCR to determine viral load
         How can you test a baby for HIV infection?
             •  Heel prick test to obtain blood from the baby
             • PCR can be done to amplify sample
         Why shouldn’t we test for antibodies in babies under two years old?
             • Do not test for antibodies as the antibodies can be transferred from
                 the mother to the child during pregnancy
                 ◦  These can stay in the child’s serum for up to two years
             •  This hence doesn’t give us an accurate representation of whether or
                 not the mother or the child has produced the antibodies in response to
                 the pathogen.

           What further tests can be done to follow-up a positive HTLV serology test?
             •  qrt-PCR to determine volume of HTLV present in patient
             •  This gives us an indication on how likely the patient is to develop
                severe complications as a result of the virus, and their likelihood of
                transmission
