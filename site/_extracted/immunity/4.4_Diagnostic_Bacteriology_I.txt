   4.4 Diagnostic Bacteriology I

         Why is diagnosis to find out which particular pathogen is the causative
            agent important?
             •  Because some diseases (i.e pneumonia) can be caused by many
                    different pathogens
             •  These pathogens are not all treated in the same way, so it is important
                  to identify the type of pathogen so we can use appropriate
                treatments for the patient.

           What colour do gram-positive bacteria stain under the gram stain?
             •  Purple
           What colour do gram-negative bacteria stain under the gram stain?
             •  Pink
             Describe the structural difference between gram-positive and gram-
             negative bacteria.
             •  Gram-positive bacteria have one outer membrane with thick
                peptidoglycan but gram-negative bacteria have two outer
             membranes with thin peptidoglycan.

             Describe the test that is done to distinguish between gram-positive and
            gram-negative bacteria.
             •  Bacteria are stained with crystal violet stain
             •  Bacteria are stained with iodine
             •  Bacteria are stained with alcohol to wash out crystal violet and iodine
             •  Bacteria are stained with safranin as the pink stain
         Why is it that gram-positive bacteria appear purple and gram-negative
              bacteria appear pink?
             • The thick peptidoglycan in gram-positive bacteria does not allow
                   crystal violet to be washed out and so the bacteria are stained purple
             • The thin peptidoglycan in gram-negative bacteria allows crystal violet
                  to be washed out and so the bacteria are stained pink by safranin
                  instead

           What are the two types of bacterial shapes?
             •  Cocci (round spheres)
             •  Bacilli (rod shaped cylinders)
           What is diplococci?
             • A pair of two cocci bacteria
           What is staphylococci?
             • A group of multiple cocci bacteria.
           What is streptococci?
             • A group of multiple cocci bacteria in a chain arrangement.
             Describe the two ways a microscope helps in diagnostic bacteriology.
             •   It helps us identify the colour that the bacteria stains in crystal violet
                  solution
             •   It helps us identify the shape of the bacteria
         How does knowing the shape and the stain colour of bacteria help us as
               clinicians?
             •   It can help us to identify the bacterial strain that is causing a particular
                   infection

           What is a hemolysin?
             • Enzymes that are released by bacteria that can damage red blood
                    cells.

           What is alpha-hemolysis?
             •  Partial hemolysis
                 ◦  green, opaque zone
           What is beta-hemolysis?
             •  Complete hemolysis
                 ◦  clear zone
           What is gamma-hemolysis?
             • No hemolysis
           What is the hemolysis test used for?
             •  Distinguishing between gram-positive bacteria such as
                 staphylococcus.

         How is the lactose test used to distinguish between bacteria?
             •  Bile salts, crystal violet and lactose are present on the agar plate
             •  Bacteria produce lactic acid when they ferment lactate during their
                growth
             •  This results in the production of pink colonies as the pH is lower.
           What is the lactose test used for?
             •  Distinguishing between gram-negative bacteria.
           What is the catalase test used for?
             •  To distinguish between streptococci and staphylococci
           What does catalase do?
             •   It is an enzyme that breaks down hydrogen peroxide into hydrogen and
                oxygen.

           What happens in the catalase test?
             •  Staphylococci produce catalase and hence bubbles
             •  Streptococci do not produce catalase and hence no bubbles are
               produced

           What is the coagulase test used for?
             •  To distinguish between staphylococcus aureus and other staphylococci
           What does coagulase do?
             •  Catalyses the conversion of prothrombin into thrombin
             •  Thrombin is then used to convert fibrinogen into fibrin
             •  Factor 8A then crosslinks fibrin to form fibrin clot
