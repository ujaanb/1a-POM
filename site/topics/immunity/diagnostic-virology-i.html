Overview

What does HTLV-1 cause?

  • HTLV-1 can cause adult T cell leukaemia (cancer)
  • It was the first virus to be found to cause malignancy
  • Approximately 5% of infected patients will develop cancer

Why is diagnosis of HTLV-1 infections sometimes difficult?

  • HTLV-1 can cause many different diseases with different symptoms, so it can be difficult to identify that HTLV-1 is responsible for symptoms seen in a patient
  • These diseases include:
  • Adult T-cell leukaemia
  • Adult T-cell leukaemia/lymphoma
  • HTLV-1-associated myelopathy
  • Tropical spastic paraparesis (TSP)
  • HTLV-1-associated infectious dermatitis
  • HTLV-1-associated uveitis

How can HTLV-1 be transmitted?

  • Mother to infant during pregnancy
  • Through sexual intercourse
  • Blood-to-blood contact (i.e transfusions/sharing needles)

Is HTLV-1 a single or double-stranded virus?

HTLV-1 is a single-stranded RNA virus

What is Tax?

  • Tax is a gene that is essential for coding for proteins useful in transcription and viral replication
  • Tax is also known to have severe oncogenic potential by accelerating the transition from the G1 to S phase as well as causing mutations which can lead to cancer.

What is the Taqman method in qrt-PCR?

  • It is an alternative method used instead of the fluorescent dye PCR method which requires the use of an oligo to bind onto the amplifying region before amplification begins
  • The oligo consists of both a fluorescent region and a quencher region and will be cleaved apart as the DNA polymerase moves along the chain
  • This means the level of fluorescence seen per cycle will be proportional to the levels of template DNA present at each point
  • i.e the more of the Taq template DNA present in the sample (i.e higher HTLV-1 load), the more oligos will be cleaved by DNA polymerase and hence the more fluorescence will be reported
  • This increases the specificity of the reaction, and although it is more expensive, this method (Taqman method) is what is commonly used for diagnostic purposes
