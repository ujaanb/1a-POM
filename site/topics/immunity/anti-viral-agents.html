Overview

How does the Zika virus infect its victims and what complications are caused?

  • A mosquito (vector) carries the virus and bites a woman during pregnancy
  • This affects the brain development of the baby and leads to microcephaly (abnormal smallness of the head)

What is the best definition of a virus?

An infectious obligate intracellular parasite.

How does a virus encode its genetic material?

Nucleic acid genome (either RNA or DNA)

What is a virion? Which is the only microscope that can be used to see viruses?

  • A viral particle.
  • Electron microscope.

What is the difference between an enveloped and non-enveloped virus?

  • An enveloped virus has an outer membrane derived from the host cell membrane
  • A non-enveloped virus has no outer membrane and it consists of purely protein.

What is the difference between a pleiomorphic and typical shaped enveloped virus?

  • A pleiomorphic virus has many shapes it can assume
  • A typical shaped virus can only assume a particular shape - making it easy to recognise.

What is a tegument?

A bunch of proteins incorporated inside the outer envelope of a virus.

What special component do viruses with RNA instead of DNA have?

Reverse transcriptase enzymes to help them convert RNA back to DNA.

Why do RNA viruses have a higher mutation rate than DNA viruses?

  • RNA viruses use their own polymerases to replicate which leads to this higher rate of mutation
  • DNA viruses use host cell machinery
  • It is an advantageous characteristic, as the viruses can evolve quicker.

What other distinction is there between DNA and RNA viruses?

  • DNA viruses are much larger than RNA viruses
  • RNA viruses are around 30 kb pairs big, DNA viruses are about 100kb pairs big.

What is negative sense RNA?

  • This is RNA derived from the complimentary strand to the mRNA strand (so tRNA)
  • Viruses that carry this form of RNA have to convert the tRNA into mRNA, which can then be translated

What is the role of an integrase enzyme in the viral replication cycle?

Integrases are used to incorporate the viral DNA into the host cell DNA genome, so that it can be transcribed together and produce the viral proteins.

What is the negative sense strand in RNA?

  • The strand that is complementary to mRNA
  • mRNA can be synthesised using the negative sense strand.

What is the cytopathic effect?

The death of a host cell caused by a virus when it replicates and causes lysis/apoptosis of the host cell.

What is a plaque?

  • It is a hole in a layer of cells that is caused by the action of a virus when it kills a host cell
  • The hole is present due to the absence of the cell that was previously in that position.

What is a syncitium?

  • A fusion of cells together
  • Viruses with surface proteins can usually form syncitia at neutral pH.
